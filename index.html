Philosophy Statement

My life purpose is to use my computer expertise and drive to build systems in financial markets to improve accessibility to all individuals and improve market data canonicalization / reporting for company-wide benefit. After the recent session, and on reflection, I find myself wanting to work within this scope based on my own past experiences, upbringing, and current interests and hobbies.

I find my perfect world to be one where I can pursue my deepest fulltime interests, where others are not hampered by limitations to access opportunities and resources within communities, such that it is replaced by equitable options for everyone. Anyone would be able to make the most of their lives and explore any path, and my effort to do so would be from a financial accessibility standpoint. In the past few years, especially since I started studying at the University of Michigan, I’ve seen a clear divide for those who can use financial resources and technologies to their advantages versus those who are either unable to, are unaware, or feel stereotyped. Being from a more well brought-up background, I want to change this for not just my peers, but for everyone in the same situation.

When pursuing this idea, I do have a few goals to attain. At the very least, I will need to have a commanding understanding of the infrastructure and design skills necessary to explore this field of software engineering. On the less technical side, other goals include identifying companies whose goal is to clear these barriers and gain insight into their current efforts and technologies to do so. Looking back 10 years from now, I hope that my efforts and focus to pursue these initiatives will pay off, whether I begin my own company or work at a pre-existing one to change it for the better.
